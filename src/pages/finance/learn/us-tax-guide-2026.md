# 2026 US Tax Guide: Why Your Refund Might Actually Be Higher (And the New Trap to Avoid)

The 2026 tax season is the first full year of the "One Big Beautiful Bill" (OBBB) impact. While the political noise has been deafening, the reality on your 1040 is what matters. 

## The Refund Reality
Early data from the IRS shows that average refunds are up about 14%. This isn't just magic; it’s a result of the restructured standard deduction and the consolidation of several niche credits into the new "Unified Family Credit." 

## The Schedule P-1 Trap
The most significant change for 2026 is the mandatory **Schedule P-1**. If you claimed any "Patriot deductions" or specific green-energy rebates in 2025, you must file this form. Failure to include it is causing automatic processing delays of up to 6 weeks. 

## Middle-Income Phase-Outs
The OBBB has very aggressive income phase-outs. If your household income crossed the $165,000 mark in 2025, you might lose eligibility for several credits you relied on in the past.
