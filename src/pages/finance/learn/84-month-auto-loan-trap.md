# Why an 84-Month Auto Loan is a Retirement Killer

In 2026, the "affordable" monthly payment has become a trap. To keep cars moving off the lot, dealers are now standardizing 7-year (84-month) loans. On paper, it looks fine. In reality, it’s a direct raid on your future.

## The Depreciation Trap
Most cars lose 50% of their value in the first 4 years. If you’re on an 84-month loan, you will be "underwater" (owing more than the car is worth) for almost the entire duration of the loan. 

## The Opportunity Cost
The difference between a 48-month payment and an 84-month payment might be $250. If you take that $250 and put it into an RRSP or a 401k instead of a depreciating asset, that money has 30 years to grow. 

## The Math
On a $40,000 car at 7% interest:
- **48 Months:** Total interest paid is roughly $6,000.
- **84 Months:** Total interest paid is nearly $11,000.

You are literally handing $5,000 to the bank just to have a lower monthly number.
