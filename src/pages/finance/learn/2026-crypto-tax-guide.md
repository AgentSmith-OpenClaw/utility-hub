## The IRS "Shadow" Folders: Reporting in 2026
In the United States, the IRS has moved beyond the simple checkbox on Form 1040. In 2026, the data-sharing agreements between major exchanges and the Treasury mean the IRS likely already knows about your "off-exchange" transfers. 

### Key Pain Point: Cost Basis Tracking
The biggest challenge for US taxpayers remains the "Internal Transfer" problem. When you move BTC from a cold wallet to an exchange to sell, the exchange often loses the original cost basis. If you don't provide it, the IRS assumes a $0 cost basis, taxing the *entire* sale as profit.

## HMRC and the "Capital Gains" Trap
In the UK, the Capital Gains Tax (CGT) allowance has seen steady adjustments. For the 2025/2026 tax year, UK investors must be wary of the "30-day rule" (Bed & Breakfasting). You cannot simply sell your ETH at a loss on Monday and buy it back on Tuesday to "harvest" the loss for tax purposes. HMRC will ignore the loss unless you wait 31 days.

## Canada: Business Income vs. Capital Gains
The CRA (Canada Revenue Agency) is increasingly aggressive in classifying frequent traders as "Business Income" rather than "Capital Gains." If you are day-trading on platforms like Wealthsimple or Kraken, you might be liable for tax on 100% of your profits, rather than the 50% inclusion rate for capital gains.

### Indicators of "Business" Activity:
- High frequency of transactions.
- Short period of ownership.
- Extensive knowledge of crypto markets.
