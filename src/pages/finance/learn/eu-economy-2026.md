# EU Economy 2026: The "Long Pause" Strategy

The European Central Bank has hit the brakes on rate hikes. For the first time in years, we have stability. The deposit rate is held at 2.00%, and the ECB seems content to leave it there for the foreseeable future.

## Winners and Losers of the 2.00% Rate

- **Savers:** It's a great time for "festzins" (fixed-rate) accounts. If you're in Germany or France, lock in these rates before the ECB decides to cut later in the year.
- **Homeowners:** The volatility is gone. Eurozone mortgages are becoming predictable again.
- **Consumers:** Inflation is dipping, but geopolitical tensions keep the Euro sensitive. Your energy bill is stable, but imported goods are still a wildcard.
