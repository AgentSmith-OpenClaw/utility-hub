# UK Energy Bills: How to Prepare for the £1,800 July Cap

The UK energy market is facing a perfect storm. The ongoing conflict in the Middle East has pushed wholesale gas prices to a three-year high, and the ripple effect is about to hit your doorstep.

## The £1,800 Reality
Market analysts are now projecting that the energy price cap will rise to approximately £1,800 per year for the average household starting in July. This is a significant jump from the relatively stable rates we saw in early 2025.

## Why it’s Happening
It’s a simple case of supply and demand, complicated by geography. With the Strait of Hormuz seeing increased military activity, the cost of transporting LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) has skyrocketed. Even though the UK doesn't get all its gas from that region, global prices are linked.

## What You Can Do
1. **Fix or Variable?** If you can find a fixed-rate deal under £1,750 right now, it might be worth locking in.
2. **Efficiency First:** The "old" advice of turning down the thermostat by one degree still saves about 10% on your bill, which matters more when the bill is £1,800 than when it was £1,200.
3. **Budget Re-alignment:** If your energy bill is going up by £20-£30 a month, something else has to give. Use our [UK Salary & Tax Calculator](/finance/salary-tax-uk) to see how your take-home pay stacks up against these new costs.

The worst thing you can do is ignore the trend. July is coming, and the prices are baked in. Start adjusting your household budget now.
