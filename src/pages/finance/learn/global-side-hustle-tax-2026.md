# Side Hustle Taxes in 2026: A Global Guide for the Modern Freelancer

The "Gig Economy" is no longer a side project—in 2026, it is a primary income source for millions across the US, UK, Canada, and Europe. But with new income comes new complexity, especially as tax authorities globally have ramped up their digital tracking systems.

Whether you are driving for a ride-share app, selling digital products, or consulting across borders, here is your 2026 survival guide for side hustle taxes.

## US: The 1099-K Threshold and New Brackets
If you are in the US, the IRS is more automated than ever. 
*   **The Threshold:** Platforms like PayPal, Venmo, and Etsy are now strictly reporting any gross income over $5,000 (a shift from the chaotic back-and-forth of previous years). 
*   **Quarterly Payments:** If you expect to owe more than $1,000 in taxes, you *must* make estimated quarterly payments to avoid penalties.

## UK: The Dividend and Capital Gains Squeeze
For UK "Side Hustlers" operating as limited companies, 2026 has brought tighter margins. The dividend allowance remains low, and capital gains tax rates have seen minor adjustments to align with income tax bands.
*   **The £1,000 Allowance:** You can still earn up to £1,000 in "trading income" tax-free before you need to register as a sole trader with HMRC.

## Canada: The GST/HST Requirement
In Canada, the magic number is still **$30,000**. Once your side hustle revenue exceeds this amount in four consecutive quarters, you must register for and collect GST/HST. 
*   **New for 2026:** The CRA is using AI-driven audits to match bank deposits with reported income. Keep your personal and business accounts separate to avoid a massive headache.

## EU: The VAT OSS Simplified
Selling digital services to customers across the EU? The **VAT One-Stop Shop (OSS)** is your best friend in 2026. It allows you to report and pay VAT for all EU countries in a single filing in your home country.
