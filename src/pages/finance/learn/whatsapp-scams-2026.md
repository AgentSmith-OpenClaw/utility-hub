# WhatsApp "Cleaning Superstore" Scams: How to Spot a 2026 Phishing Attack

In 2026, the scammers have moved on from "Hi Mom" texts to something much more sophisticated: the "Missed Delivery" loop.

## The Setup
You get a WhatsApp message from a brand that looks legitimate—"Cleaning Superstore" or "Global Logistics Hub." It says you have a package waiting, but the delivery address is incomplete. It includes a link to "verify your details."

## Why It Works
Scammers are using AI to mimic the exact tone and timing of actual retailers. If you’ve ordered anything online recently (and who hasn't?), the message feels plausible.

## The Red Flags
1. **The URL:** If it’s not the official website of the company, don't click. Scammers use "cleaningsuperstore-verify.com" instead of "cleaningsuperstore.com."
2. **The Urgency:** They claim the package will be "returned to sender" in 2 hours. This is a classic psychological trick to stop you from thinking clearly.
3. **The WhatsApp Origin:** Major retailers rarely use WhatsApp for initial delivery notifications unless you’ve specifically opted in.
