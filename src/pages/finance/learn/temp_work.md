# 2026 US Tax Sunset: The TCJA Cliff is Here

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017 fundamentally changed the US tax landscape. However, many of its individual provisions were temporary. As we move through 2026, we are staring down the "sunset" of these cuts. If Congress doesn't act, most Americans will see a tax hike starting in 2027. This guide explains what is expiring and how you can prepare now.

## What is Expiring?

The TCJA lower tax brackets are scheduled to revert to their pre-2018 levels. This means the 12% bracket goes back to 15%, the 22% becomes 25%, and the top rate moves from 37% to 39.6%. Furthermore, the Standard Deduction—which was nearly doubled by the TCJA—will be cut roughly in half, adjusted for inflation.

### Key Changes to Watch:
- **Tax Brackets:** Rates will increase across the board.
- **Standard Deduction:** Significant reduction, making itemization more common again.
- **Child Tax Credit:** Will likely drop from $2,000 to $1,000 per child.
- **SALT Deduction:** The $10,000 cap on State and Local Tax deductions might expire, which actually helps residents in high-tax states like CA and NY.

## Strategic Planning for 2026

Since rates are lower now than they are expected to be in 2027, the general strategy is to **accelerate income** into 2026 and **defer deductions** until 2027.

1. **Roth Conversions:** If you have a traditional IRA, 2026 might be the last year to convert it to a Roth IRA at these lower rates. You pay the tax now to avoid higher taxes later.
2. **Capital Gains:** If you were planning to sell assets, doing so in 2026 ensures you lock in current long-term capital gains rates.
3. **Charitable Giving:** If the standard deduction drops in 2027, your charitable contributions might actually be deductible again. Consider "bunching" donations in 2027 instead of 2026.

# UK Mortgage Crisis 2026: Avoiding the SVR Trap

For years, UK homeowners enjoyed record-low interest rates. But in 2026, the landscape has shifted. With the Bank of England maintaining a "higher for longer" stance to combat persistent inflation, hundreds of thousands of fixed-rate deals are expiring in Q2 2026.

## The Standard Variable Rate (SVR) Danger

When your fixed-rate mortgage ends, your lender automatically moves you to their Standard Variable Rate (SVR). In 2026, SVRs are often 2-3% higher than the best available fixed or tracker rates. For a £300,000 mortgage, that difference can mean an extra £500 per month in interest alone.

## Your 2026 Refinancing Strategy

1. **Start Early:** You can usually lock in a new rate 6 months before your current deal expires. If you are in the Q2 2026 cohort, you should be looking at deals right now.
2. **Consider Trackers:** If you believe the BoE will start cutting rates in late 2026, a tracker mortgage might save you more in the long run than a 5-year fix.
3. **Overpayment:** If you have spare cash, overpaying your mortgage before the rate resets can reduce your Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio, potentially qualifying you for a better tier of interest rates.

# Canada FHSA vs RRSP: The 2026 First-Time Buyer Strategy

The First Home Savings Account (FHSA) has become the gold standard for Canadian home buyers. In 2026, the rules around stacking the FHSA with the Home Buyers' Plan (HBP) via your RRSP have been further refined.

## Stacking for Maximum Impact

In 2026, a couple can potentially have $16,000 in annual FHSA contribution room ($8,000 each). Combined with the RRSP HBP limit (which remains at $60,000 as of recent updates), a couple could theoretically pull over $100,000 for a down payment—all tax-free.

## Why FHSA Wins in 2026
Unlike the RRSP HBP, funds taken from an FHSA for a qualifying home purchase **do not need to be paid back**. This makes the FHSA the single most powerful tool in the Canadian tax code for wealth generation and housing.

## The 2026 Deadline
Remember, FHSA room does not carry forward indefinitely like an RRSP. If you don't open the account, you don't start accruing the $8,000 annual room. Even if you only put $10 in, open the account in 2026 to start the clock.

## What it Means for Borrowers

# High-Yield Savings vs Dividend Stocks: The 2026 Verdict

In 2026, the "yield gap"—the difference between what a bank pays you in a savings account and what a stable company pays you in dividends—has narrowed.

## The Case for Cash (HYSA)
With market volatility increasing due to geopolitical shifts, keeping a portion of your portfolio in a 4.5% HYSA remains a rational "sleep-at-night" strategy. It is liquid, insured, and predictable.

## The Case for Dividends
However, dividend-growing stocks (the "Dividend Aristocrats") offer something cash cannot: capital appreciation and inflation protection. In 2026, high-quality dividend payers are yielding 3.5% to 5%, often with the added benefit of qualified tax treatment in the US.

## Recommendation
For an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses), use a HYSA. For long-term wealth building, don't let the allure of high cash rates distract you from the power of compounding equities.
